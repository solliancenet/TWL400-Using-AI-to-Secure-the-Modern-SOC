# Task 01.03 — Extract entities and assemble the initial timeline

## Introduction

A narrative is persuasive; a list of entities and an ordered timeline are *actionable*. Before you reconstruct the attack in Exercise 02, you need firm anchors — the specific users, devices, resources, and indicators, in the order they appear — so your hunting queries have something concrete to pivot on.

## Description

You will extract the key entities from the incident and the Copilot narrative, and assemble an initial, ordered timeline of the attack.

## Success Criteria

- Key actors and indicators are captured _(Validation: entities extracted)_
- An ordered initial timeline is recorded _(Validation: timeline extracted)_
- The entity list spans all five stages (identity, email/poisoning, AI, infrastructure, endpoint)

### 02: Assemble the initial timeline

Put the entities in order.

<details markdown="block">
<summary><strong>Expand this section for detailed steps</strong></summary>

1. Ask Copilot to order the events:

   ```text
   Build a chronological timeline of this incident. For each event give the
   timestamp, the layer, the entity, and a one-line description.
   ```

2. Look at the **Timestamp** column — every row shows the **same time**. That's a deliberate limitation of the lab's seeding: all events are ingested at once, so they share a single `TimeGenerated`, and the incident's **First activity** and **Last activity** are identical. There is **no temporal spread**, so Copilot can't order the events by clock time and its "timeline" may not be in attack order. The true sequence comes from the **attack's logic**, not the timestamps — identity compromise → poisoned grounding (email) → AI prompt-injection → infrastructure reach → endpoint execution. Copilot's output will resemble (timestamps will all match your ingestion time):

   | Timestamp | Layer | Entity | Description |
   |-----------|-------|--------|-------------|
   | 2026-06-26 16:05:41 | Identity | `mariya.petrova@AcesaDev.onmicrosoft.com` | Suspicious credential access activity detected for user account. |
   | 2026-06-26 16:05:41 | Service Principal | `sp-refund-agent-inference` | Service account implicated in credential access activity. |
   | 2026-06-26 16:05:41 | Endpoint | `OPS-LT-0427` | Host device involved in the cross-layer attack sequence. |
   | 2026-06-26 16:05:41 | AI Application/Cloud | `refund-agent-prod` (Azure resource) | Azure AI resource targeted as part of the coordinated attack. |

   {: .note }
   > This is an **initial** timeline. Because every event carries the same ingestion timestamp, you can't reconstruct true wall-clock ordering here — the sequence is inferred from the attack's logic, not the data. In Exercise 02 you'll hunt the underlying `SocLabEvents_CL` telemetry with KQL, where each event's **`Stage`** field (`1-Identity` → `5-Endpoint`) gives the ordering the timestamps can't. It still has gaps and assumptions — you'll confirm and tighten it there.

</details>

### 03: Save the anchors

Record what reconstruction will pivot on.

<details markdown="block">
<summary><strong>Expand this section for detailed steps</strong></summary>

1. Save the entity table and the initial timeline to your running notes.

2. Flag the two entities most likely to unlock the rest: **Mariya Petrova** (identity pivot) and **`sp-refund-agent-inference`** (infrastructure pivot). You'll lead with these in Exercise 02's hunting.

</details>

## Summary

You now have a concrete entity list spanning all five stages and an initial timeline ordered by the attack's logic. These anchors turn the next exercise from open-ended hunting into targeted reconstruction. Before you move on, look back at the raw queue one more time — ~25 decoys with the chain laced through it — and articulate why a human analyst would have missed all of this.
