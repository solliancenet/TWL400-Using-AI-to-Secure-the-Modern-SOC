# Task 02 - Confirm suspicious sign-in activity and lateral movement (Defender for Identity)

## Introduction

Your KQL hunt placed all five stages, but two of them — the **identity compromise** (Stage 1) and the **lateral movement toward AI infrastructure** (Stage 4) — are where Defender for Identity adds detail the raw tables can't. Defender for Identity reconstructs an *account-centric* story: it builds a per-user timeline, surfaces the Entra ID Protection risk detection for the suspicious sign-in, and traces how a compromised identity moves. Confirming Stages 1 and 4 here corroborates your reconstruction with a second, independent view and gives you the entity timelines you'll attach to the response actions in Exercise 03.

## Description

You will use Defender for Identity inside the unified SecOps portal to confirm the **Stage 1 anonymous IP sign-in** for Mariya Petrova and the **Stage 4 lateral movement** by `sp-refund-agent-inference` toward the AI/GPU inference infrastructure. You'll read each entity's timeline and alerts and reconcile what they show against the entities you captured in Task 02.01.

## Success criteria

- You have opened Mariya Petrova's identity timeline and confirmed the anonymous IP sign-in from `102.89.42.17` _(Validation: Stage 1 anonymous IP detection confirmed in Defender for Identity)_
- You have confirmed the Stage 4 lateral-movement activity for `sp-refund-agent-inference` reaching toward `rg-soclab` _(Validation: Stage 4 lateral movement confirmed)_
- The entities match the lifecycle you assembled in Task 02.01 (and you've noted any discrepancies to resolve)

### 02: Confirm the Stage 1 anonymous IP sign-in

Verify the sign-in from anonymous infrastructure and link it to the attacker IP.

<details markdown="block">
<summary><strong>Expand this section to view the solution</strong></summary>

1. Open the **Timeline** tab for Mariya and set the range to the incident window. Locate the sign-in from `102.89.42.17` — flagged by Entra ID Protection as an **`anonymousIpAddress`** detection. Defender for Identity surfaces this on the account timeline as the same Stage 1 identity event you found in `SocLabEvents_CL`: an AiTM-stolen session token replayed from an IP classified as anonymizing infrastructure (Tor exit node or known anonymous proxy) in Microsoft threat intelligence.

2. Select the related alert (for example, **Anonymous IP address** from Entra ID Protection) and read its evidence: the suspicious IP, the risk level assigned by Microsoft threat intelligence, and the apps accessed in the flagged session. Confirm the **medium** severity — high enough to be real, low enough to get buried.

3. Pivot on the IP `102.89.42.17` to see everything it touched. Confirm it is associated **only** with the attacker session and not with Mariya's normal pattern — this bounds the identity stage's blast radius to the AiTM-stolen session.

4. Reconcile against Task 02.01: the IP and user should match your Stage 1 entry. Note any discrepancies so they don't read as contradictions later.

   > 📷 **Screenshot to capture:** the anonymous IP sign-in alert on Mariya's timeline showing the Entra ID Protection risk detection, the flagged IP `102.89.42.17`, and the assigned risk level.

### 03: Confirm the Stage 4 lateral movement toward AI infrastructure

Trace the service principal's reach toward the GPU inference infrastructure.

<details markdown="block">
<summary><strong>Expand this section to view the solution</strong></summary>

1. From the incident (or **Assets**), open the service principal **`sp-refund-agent-inference`** _(illustrative — confirm at build)_. Review its identity page and **Timeline** for the incident window.

2. Confirm the lateral-movement signal: anomalous authentication / token activity and the reach toward `rg-soclab` (the lab resource group hosting `oai-soclab-v02`, the Azure OpenAI resource serving as Zava's AI inference infrastructure in this environment). Defender for Identity flags this as suspicious because it deviates from the service principal's established behavior — the move from "compromised identity" to "reaching high-value infrastructure."

3. Walk the connection back to the identity compromise: confirm the chain from Mariya's stolen session → the service-principal activity → the inference infrastructure. If a lateral-movement path is rendered, follow it; it visualizes how the attacker pivoted toward the AI workload. This is the corroboration that Stage 4 is genuine lateral movement, not routine automation.

4. Reconcile against Task 02.01: the service principal should match your Stage 4 entry from `SocLabEvents_CL` — note the Stage 4 event names the GPU compute cluster but not a specific resource group; that is expected.

   {: .warning }
   > **Build note:** how a *service principal's* lateral movement renders in Defender for Identity (full timeline vs. an alert reference vs. correlation into the unified incident) is one of the open build decisions. If the dedicated lateral-movement-path view isn't available for a service principal in the provisioned tenant, capture the corroboration from the unified incident's correlated alerts plus your `SocLabEvents_CL` Stage 4 evidence instead. The `sp-refund-agent-inference` service principal has Reader and Cognitive Services User access on `rg-soclab` (the lab resource group hosting `oai-soclab-v02`) — this is the actual scope reflected in DFI activity.

   {: .note }
   > 📷 **Screenshot to capture:** the `sp-refund-agent-inference` timeline/alert showing the anomalous activity and its reach toward `rg-soclab`.

</details>
