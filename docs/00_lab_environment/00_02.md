# Task 00.02 — Orient to the scenario and the SOC alert queue

## Introduction

Zava's SOC is not failing because it lacks tools — it has 50–75 of them. It is failing because those tools each raise individually low-severity alerts in separate consoles, and no one is correlating them. The queue is flooded: roughly **25 decoy incidents** — routine risky sign-ins, benign DLP hits, commodity malware, failed-logon bursts, impossible-but-benign travel — with the five-stage attack scattered among them. Before you start investigating, look at the queue the way Zava's overwhelmed analysts see it, so you understand the problem you're being asked to prove.

## Description

You will review the scenario and your role, scan the incident and alert queue to feel the signal volume, and confirm the deliverable you're working toward — the **Proof Through Scenario**.

## Success Criteria

- You can restate the scenario, your role, and the five-stage attack in one or two sentences
- You have scanned the noisy incident/alert queue (~25 decoy incidents) and noted the volume and spread across products
- You understand that the attack chain is buried among the decoys and must be hunted out
- You know the artifact you will hand to Modules 4 and 5

## Key Tasks

### 01: Read the scenario and your role

Ground yourself in who you are and what's at stake.

<details markdown="block">
<summary><strong>Expand this section for detailed steps</strong></summary>

You are a security Cloud Solution Architect advising **Zava**. Zava's leadership cast includes **Vikram** (CISO), **Michelle** (CAIO), **Phillip** (Identity & SecOps), **Nancy** (Data Security & Governance), **Amy** (App/AI Security), and **Quinton** (Infrastructure). They are under board pressure to scale AI safely — and they suspect, but cannot prove, that something has gone wrong around the **Zava Refund Agent**, an internal RAG-grounded AI assistant running on a real Azure OpenAI endpoint.

A coordinated, AI-driven attack has moved across five layers in roughly 75 minutes:

| Stage | Layer | What happened |
|-------|-------|---------------|
| 1 | Identity | Adversary-in-the-middle phishing stole a session token; Entra ID Protection flagged the subsequent sign-in as an **anonymous IP address** (Tor exit node / anonymizing infrastructure) |
| 2 | Email / poisoning | A phishing email planted a poisoned "policy update" document in the Refund Agent's knowledge base |
| 3 | AI | The Refund Agent was probed with prompt-injection and jailbreak attempts |
| 4 | Infrastructure | Anomalous requests moved toward the GPU inference infrastructure |
| 5 | Endpoint | A suspicious script executed on a workstation |

Your job: **prove what happened, show why detection failed, and demonstrate the response that should have fired.**

</details>

### 02: Scan the alert queue and feel the overload

See the noise before you cut through it.

<details markdown="block">
<summary><strong>Expand this section for detailed steps</strong></summary>

1. Back in the Defender portal, open **Incidents & alerts > Incidents**. Note the volume — roughly **25 incidents** in the queue, the overwhelming majority of them decoys (routine risky sign-ins, benign DLP hits, commodity malware, failed-logon bursts, impossible-but-benign travel).

2. Switch to **Incidents & alerts > Alerts**. Sort by **Severity** and note how many alerts are **Low** or **Informational** — the attack signals are deliberately not the loudest things in the queue.

3. Group or filter by **Detection source** and notice the spread — Entra ID Protection, Defender for Office 365, Defender for Identity, Defender for Endpoint, and **Microsoft Defender for Cloud — AI threat protection** (the surface that raised a real alert on the Zava Refund Agent's Azure OpenAI endpoint) each contributed signals.

4. Notice that no single alert screams "full compromise," and the five attack-chain signals are scattered, randomly timed, among the ~25 decoys. This fragmentation — the right signals buried in noise across the wrong number of places — **is** the problem you're proving. Your job in Exercise 01 is to cut that chain out of the decoys.

</details>
