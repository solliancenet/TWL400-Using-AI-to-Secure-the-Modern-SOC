# Task 04 - Package the Proof Through Scenario artifact

## Introduction

You have the three components: the end-to-end visualization (Task 04.01), the failure-point summary (Task 04.02), and the business-impact narrative (Task 04.03). On their own they're three files. The module's deliverable is one coherent artifact — the **Proof Through Scenario** — that a presenter can walk through in a single sitting and that Modules 4 and 5 can build on without reopening the lab. This final task assembles and packages it.

## Description

You will combine the visualization, the failure-point summary, and the business-impact narrative into the single **Proof Through Scenario** deliverable, choose a presentation format, and confirm it's ready to hand off to the operating-model (Module 4) and executive-value (Module 5) modules.

## Success criteria

- You have assembled a single artifact containing all three components — end-to-end visualization, failure-point summary, and business-impact narrative
- The artifact opens with a one-paragraph executive summary and is readable by a non-analyst without the lab telemetry
- The package is explicitly ready for hand-off to Module 4 (SOC operating model) and Module 5 (executive value) _(Validation: Proof Through Scenario deliverable produced)_

## Key tasks

### 01: Generate the executive summary

Write the cover paragraph that frames the whole artifact.

<details markdown="block">
<summary><strong>Expand this section to view the solution</strong></summary>

1. Have Security Copilot draft the opening summary that ties the three components together:

   ```text
   Write a one-paragraph executive summary of this incident and our findings for a
   CISO and CAIO. State that a five-stage cross-layer attack reached production AI
   assets in about 75 minutes, that the current SOC missed it because the signals
   were fragmented and uncorrelated across 50–75 tools, and that an AI-driven SOC
   detects and contains the same chain. No jargon, no product list.
   ```

2. Tighten the result so it stands alone — someone who reads only this paragraph should understand the attack, the failure, and the fix.

   {: .note }
   > 📷 **Screenshot to capture:** the Copilot-generated executive summary paragraph.

</details>

### 02: Assemble the package contents

Put the three components in order behind the summary.

<details markdown="block">
<summary><strong>Expand this section to view the solution</strong></summary>

1. Assemble the **Proof Through Scenario** in this order — it reads as "here's the story, here's why we missed it, here's what it costs and what changes":

   | # | Component | Source | Purpose |
   |---|-----------|--------|---------|
   | 0 | Executive summary | Task 04.04 (this task) | One paragraph: attack, failure, fix |
   | 1 | End-to-end attack visualization | Task 04.01 | The five-stage cross-layer chain in one picture |
   | 2 | Failure-point summary | Task 04.02 | What detection/response broke down, and the AI blind spot |
   | 3 | Business-impact narrative | Task 04.03 | SOC-overload impact + the AI-driven fix, framed for CISO and CAIO |

2. Confirm consistency across the components: the same five stages, the same canon entities, and the same ~75-minute dwell time appear in each. Inconsistencies between the picture and the narrative will undercut the artifact in front of executives.

   {: .warning }
   > Every seeded entity, timestamp, and portal detail in this package is **illustrative** until validated against the provisioned lab tenant. Confirm them at build before the artifact is used outside the lab.

</details>

### 03: Choose the format and finalize for hand-off

Decide how it's delivered and confirm it's ready for Modules 4 and 5.

<details markdown="block">
<summary><strong>Expand this section to view the solution</strong></summary>

1. Choose a delivery format suited to an executive audience. A recommended package is **a Copilot-generated executive summary + the cross-layer diagram + the failure-point log**, delivered as either:

   - a short slide deck (one slide per component — summary, diagram, failures, impact), or
   - a one- to two-page brief with the diagram inline.

   A Security Copilot promptbook is a convenient way to regenerate the summary and failure log consistently for future incidents.

2. Confirm the hand-off. The artifact feeds the next two modules directly:

   - **Module 4 (SOC operating model)** — the failure-point summary and the autonomy boundary from Exercise 03 become the inputs for redesigning how the SOC detects and responds.
   - **Module 5 (executive value)** — the visualization and business-impact narrative become the spine of the value case for Vikram and Michelle.

   {: .tip }
   > The test of a finished Proof Through Scenario: hand it to someone who never opened the lab. If they can explain what happened, why it was missed, and what to do about it, the artifact is ready.

   {: .note }
   > 📷 **Screenshot to capture:** the assembled Proof Through Scenario package — executive summary, diagram, failure-point log, and impact narrative together.

</details>

Congratulations! You've packaged the end-to-end visualization, the failure-point summary, and the business-impact narrative into a single **Proof Through Scenario** artifact, fronted by an executive summary and ready to hand off. That completes the module's core deliverable: concrete proof that Zava's current SOC missed a cross-layer attack on its AI assets and that an AI-driven SOC catches and contains it — the evidence Module 4 uses to redesign the operating model and Module 5 uses to make the executive case.
